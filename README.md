# Community Behaviour and Participation Analysis for Welfare Outreach

## NSS Open Projects 2026

### Problem Statement

Track 5 – Data Analytics & Insights

Challenge 5.2 – Community Behaviour & Participation Analysis on Welfare Schemes

---

## Objective

This project identifies districts that may require targeted welfare outreach by analysing demographic and socio-economic indicators from the Census 2011 dataset.

The study develops a Welfare Participation Score using literacy, workforce participation, and higher education indicators. Districts are segmented into different priority categories to identify regions that may face challenges in accessing welfare schemes.

---

## Dataset

Source:
India Census 2011 District-Level Dataset

Records:
640 districts

Features:

* Population
* Literacy
* Workforce participation
* Educational attainment
* Gender distribution
* Religious demographics

---

## Methodology

1. Data Cleaning
2. Exploratory Data Analysis
3. Feature Engineering

   * Literacy Rate
   * Worker Rate
   * Female Ratio
   * Graduate Rate
4. Welfare Participation Score Construction
5. District Segmentation
6. Intervention Prioritisation

---

## Key Findings

* Significant variation exists across Indian districts.
* Literacy and educational attainment strongly influence participation indicators.
* Several districts in Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Haryana and Jammu & Kashmir show relatively low participation scores.
* Targeted awareness campaigns and enrolment support programs can improve welfare outreach efficiency.
